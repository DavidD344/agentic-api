Neste contexto, a Avaliação do Ciclo de Vida (ACV) é uma ferramenta quantitativa essencial para mensurar impactos ambientais de produtos e processos, apoiando a tomada de decisão em sustentabilidade. No entanto, a ACV tradicional é intensiva em tempo e recursos, e os dados para sua realização são escassos, fragmentados e heterogêneos. Adicionalmente, embora a Inteligência Artificial (IA) ofereça vasto potencial para otimizar processos , a quantificação dos seus próprios impactos ambientais é uma lacuna crítica, com poucos trabalhos focados em estimar os impactos da integração IA-ACV. Este projeto de pesquisa propõe desenvolver e aplicar metodologias inovadoras baseadas em IA e Aprendizado de Máquina (AM) para otimizar processos de geração, expansão, inferência e padronização de dados em ACV, bem como apoiar a elaboração e revisão de Declarações Ambientais de Produto (EPDs), visando maior eficiência, agilidade e confiabilidade na tomada de decisão ambiental. O projeto será estruturado em quatro linhas de atuação principais: (i) Expansão e Construção Automatizada de Bases de Dados de ACV; (ii) Uso de Modelos de IA para Inferência em Inventários e Impactos Ambientais; (iii) Otimização do Processo de Desenvolvimento e Revisão de EPDs; e (iv) Avaliação dos Impactos Ambientais da IA Aplicada à ACV. Este projeto é inovador no contexto brasileiro, preenchendo uma lacuna de pesquisa e oferecendo soluções tangíveis para acelerar a gestão da sustentabilidade e enfrentar a Tríplice Crise Planetária. Ele representa uma contribuição inovadora e estratégica para a Engenharia e Gestão da Sustentabilidade, preenchendo uma lacuna crítica na literatura e na prática. Os resultados esperados incluem procedimentos e softwares registrados, publicações em periódicos de alto impacto, estudos de caso com empresas parceiras e a promoção de uma tomada de decisão mais ágil e confiável. Descrição: (CNPq 443899/2024-0) Many diseases have ineffective treatments, resulting in high mortality rates, which makes the discovery of new therapies essential for humanity. Latent knowledge discovery involves the automated identification of elements that can provide new insights that would be difficult to achieve using traditional methods. However, the automation of such a process is limited by the strong dependence on experts, which can be compensated by using AutoML methods. The most significant limitation of these methods lies in the lack of transparency of the generated models, representing a substantial obstacle, especially in critical areas such as healthcare.This project proposes a multimodal approach that combines AutoML, NLP and explainability techniques to overcome current limitations and provide faster and more understandable discoveries in the medical field. By integrating structured data, such as clinical and genetic information, with unstructured data, such as medical texts, this approach can uncover patterns that can accelerate the discovery of new therapies and/or make existing treatments more effective.Recent advances in AutoML and explainability techniques suggest that this strategy can enable the discovery of new knowledge and ensure the transparency needed for application in critical decision systems. The project aims to use these techniques to generate complementary views of data that can accelerate the development of new therapies, contributing significantly to improving medical treatments and, consequently, to the health and survival of patients..

Video Understanding through Deep Learning with Minimal Human Supervision

Projeto certificado pelo(a) coordenador(a) Jurandy Gomes de Almeida Junior em 29/08/2024.
Descrição: (FAPESP 2023/17577-0) Digital videos have become the medium of choice for a growing number of people communicating via Internet and their mobile devices. Over the past decade, world has witnessed an explosive growth in the amount of video data fostered by astonishing technological developments. In this scenario, there is a growing demand for efficient systems to reduce the work and information overload for people. Making efficient use of video content requires the development of intelligent tools capable to understand videos in a similar way as humans do. This has been the goal of a quickly evolving research area known as video understanding. Last years, thanks to its great learning capacity from data exposure, deep learning has led to remarkable progress in video understanding. Largely, this is due to the availability of large amounts of labeled data that has contributed to the development of models with extraordinary inference capabilities. Acquiring and annotating a desirable amount of data to satisfy these models is often a hard task for many application domains, requiring overwhelming human effort and, sometimes, specific expertise. This reliance on exhaustive labeling is a key limitation to deploying video understanding systems in the real world. Although deep learning models excel in many video understanding tasks, occasionally beating humans, their generalization ability is poor, only performing tasks they are trained for. The ability to adapt to novel scenarios is the hallmark of human intelligence. Motivated by such aspects, this research proposal aims to investigate methods to improve the generalization ability of deep learning, enabling to perform video understanding with minimal human supervision. The main scientific contribution will be a new and more sustainable pipeline for deep learning applications, requiring tailored human knowledge to disambiguate only critical decisions. For this, we intend to advance the state of the art in model generalization, aiming to learn with most informative data and put the human in the loop in a more effective manner. Finally, the results of this research proposal are also meant to enable companies to put AI into their products or production systems reducing the need for experienced operator knowledge for data annotation..

Modelo de predição de impactos ambientais de produtos baseado na Avaliação do Ciclo de Vida integrada à Inteligência Artificial
Descrição: (FAPESP 2022/15134-1) Studies reveal that 78 of American consumers and 70 of Brazilian consumers already value more sustainable lifestyles, mainly due to personal well-being and concern for future generations. However, climate risks are becoming increasingly imminent and complex to solve, requiring quick and accurate responses. In this context, the importance of integrating Life Cycle Assessment (LCA) and Machine Learning (ML) techniques emerges to support environmental decisions quickly and reliably. LCA, regulated by ISO 14040 and ISO 14044, demands time and large volumes of data for its execution. On the other hand, ML, with its ability to process data and generate predictive models, offers a solution to reduce the analysis time without compromising the quality of results. This study aims to propose and test decision support models using machine learning trained with LCA datasets to predict environmental impacts more efficiently. This exploratory research is divided into two main articles. The first article reviews the literature on computational techniques and modeling languages suitable for predicting environmental impacts, aiming to evaluate the state of LCA+ML integration. Finally, the second article applies ML models to national and global datasets used in LCA of national agribusiness products as a case study, comparing the results and proposing a final decision support model. The methods adopted involve using both parameterized and non-parameterized LCA databases, such as ecoinvent, Agribalyse, Agri-footprint, and SICV Brazil, as well as supervised and unsupervised machine learning techniques. The main challenge of this research was dealing with the heterogeneity of LCA data, as different databases use varying formats, such as ecoSpold and ILCD, impacting interoperability. Open-source software, such as OpenLCA, was used to ensure data compatibility and portability. It is expected that the conclusive results of this research will lead to an efficient decision support model capable of predicting environmental impacts in a reduced time, offering a valuable tool for companies and consumers seeking to incorporate sustainable practices into their decision-making processes. Additionally, it is hoped that this research will contribute to the evolution of LCA and ML integration techniques, offering a replicable approach for different sectors and contexts..

Sustainable Deep Learning for the Development of Autonomous Computational Intelligences

Projeto certificado pelo(a) coordenador(a) Jurandy Gomes de Almeida Junior em 06/12/2023.
Descrição: (CNPq 420442/2023-5) This project aims to develop science and technology to enable the implementation of Autonomous Computational Intelligences (ACIs). ACIs are software and hardware systems that, in combination with sensors and actuators, form intelligent entities capable of autonomously performing complex tasks in the physical or virtual world. They are capable of interacting with humans, machines and systems, such as traffic, to perform complex tasks, for instance, the delivery of products by an autonomous truck controlled by an ACI. Recently, thanks to the significant advances introduced by deep learning, neural networks have become essential towards the development of powerful ACIs. This deep learning revolution has been important to build trust in such a technology. However, the need to incorporate such knowledge in low computing power devices requires attention regarding processing speed and memory consumption. Moreover, acquiring and annotating problem-specific real-world datasets, especially in robotics and autonomous systems, is often a hard task requiring overwhelming human effort and, sometimes, specific expertise. Although the development of ACIs has advanced a lot with deep learning, it still requires cutting-edge computers to run models and process sensor data as well as expensive human annotation. Motivated by these aspects, this project proposes to investigate methods to reduce the computational and human burden of deep learning in order to enable the development of more powerful ACIs . This proposal aims to put forward a new and more sustainable pipeline for deep learning applications. Multimodal Automated Machine Learning Framework for Latent Knowledge Discovery in Medicine
Descrição: (CNPq 443899/2024-0) Many diseases have ineffective treatments, resulting in high mortality rates, which makes the discovery of new therapies essential for humanity. However, the development of new therapies is a time-consuming and costly process. ML methods can potentially accelerate discoveries by identifying latent knowledge in large volumes of medical data.Latent knowledge can be defined as relationships or patterns hidden in large data volumes. Latent knowledge discovery involves the automated identification of elements that can provide new insights that would be difficult to achieve using traditional methods. However, the automation of such a process is limited by the strong dependence on experts, which can be compensated by using AutoML methods. The most significant limitation of these methods lies in the lack of transparency of the generated models, representing a substantial obstacle, especially in critical areas such as healthcare.This project proposes a multimodal approach that combines AutoML, NLP and explainability techniques to overcome current limitations and provide faster and more understandable discoveries in the medical field. By integrating structured data, such as clinical and genetic information, with unstructured data, such as medical texts, this approach can uncover patterns that can accelerate the discovery of new therapies and/or make existing treatments more effective.Recent advances in AutoML and explainability techniques suggest that this strategy can enable the discovery of new knowledge and ensure the transparency needed for application in critical decision systems. The project aims to use these techniques to generate complementary views of data that can accelerate the development of new therapies, contributing significantly to improving medical treatments and, consequently, to the health and survival of patients..
